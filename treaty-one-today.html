Modern Treaty One

Today, Treaty One Nations are not only preserving their history but actively shaping the future. The First Nations within Treaty 1 continue to strengthen their governments, communities, and cultural identities in modern Canada.

Modern Treaty One is about more than the original 1871 agreement; it represents ongoing self-determination, leadership, and progress for the seven Nations within the territory.

Development Projects

In recent years, Treaty One Nations have become increasingly involved in major land and business initiatives throughout Manitoba.

These projects reflect a growing focus on urban expansion, partnerships, and creating new opportunities both within and beyond their communities.

Many developments are designed to support long-term economic growth while also benefiting future generations of Treaty One people.

Treaty One Development Corporation (TIDC) is established by the seven Chiefs of TIN.

Kapyong Barracks

Kapyong Barracks is a former Canadian military base located in Winnipeg, Manitoba. After the land was declared surplus by the federal government, Treaty One Nations began negotiations to reclaim the site.

Following a series of Federal Court and Court of Appeal decisions (SEPT 2009 – AUG 2015) , the Federal Court of Appeal ruled that Canada failed to properly consult with First Nations before the sale of the Kapyong Barracks. As a result of the Federal Court of Appeal ruling, Treaty 1 First Nations were able to obtain the land through purchase, eventually signing the Comprehensive Settlement Agreement with Canada.

The land agreement was finalized in 2019 after years of legal discussions and advocacy, marking an important moment for Treaty One Nations and urban Indigenous development in Manitoba. The site was formally repatriated as the Naawi‑Oodena urban reserve in 2022.

Today, the Kapyong Barracks site is planned to become a major development area that includes housing, businesses, green spaces, and community services.

Location

Kapyong Barracks is located in the southwest region of Winnipeg, Manitoba, near the intersection of Kenaston Boulevard and Grant Avenue.

The site is positioned close to several major roads, shopping areas, schools, and residential neighborhoods, making it one of the most valuable and well-connected areas in the city.

Its location also places it near important transportation routes, allowing easy access to other parts of Winnipeg and contributing to its significance as a future development project.
